Be careful with messages asking for money or codes

This page is used to help detect and document suspicious activity. If you arrived here from a chat message or email, there is a chance that someone is trying to trick you into sending money, codes, or personal information.

What you should not do

  • Do not share OTPs, passwords, PINs, or card details with anyone.
  • Do not send money, gift cards, or crypto to people you do not fully trust.
  • Do not open attachments or install apps sent by unknown contacts.
  • Do not let anyone control your device remotely unless you initiated the support.

What you should do

  • Verify any request directly with your bank or the official company.
  • Talk to someone you trust before sending money or codes.
  • Take screenshots of suspicious chats or emails as evidence.
  • Report serious fraud attempts to your local cybercrime or consumer protection agency.

Technical note: when you open this page, standard technical data (such as your IP address, browser type, and visit time) may be logged for security analysis and to help build a record of suspicious activity. This page does not access your camera, microphone, or precise location.
